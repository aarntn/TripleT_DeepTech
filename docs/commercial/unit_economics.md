# SolarGuard Unit Economics

## Baseline Contract

SolarGuard prices around a combined hardware and software O&M system cost of RM 120,000 per MW. For a 5 MW Malaysia deployment, this gives a RM 600,000 system cost and a recurring cleaning-system O&M assumption of RM 15,000 per MW per year.

## Revenue Recovery

The commercial case comes from recovered solar output that would otherwise be lost to dust and soiling. The Malaysia baseline assumes 4.5 kWh/m2/day irradiance and RM 0.35/kWh. The GCC case assumes 5.8 kWh/m2/day and RM 0.42/kWh with a higher dust penalty.

## Payback And NPV

The model uses a 10% discount rate and 7-year project life. Payback is calculated as upfront system cost divided by annual net benefit, where annual net benefit is recovered energy revenue minus annual O&M.

## Carbon Credits

Carbon value uses a RM 40/tonne baseline and 0.000585 tCO2/kWh avoided grid emission factor. Carbon credits are additive upside and are shown separately from recovered electricity revenue.

## Pricing Strategy

Early pilots should focus on revenue-share or fixed O&M contracts for 5-20 MW farms where soiling losses are material and avoided manual cleaning cost is visible. Malaysia deployments can emphasize water self-supply, while GCC deployments should emphasize high dust recovery and higher irradiance.
