# SolarGuard: Malaysian Funder & Partner Landscape

**Purpose:** Research-backed map of realistic funders, grant providers, pilot partners, and strategic investors for SolarGuard in Malaysia.

**Research date:** 2026-05-16

**Evidence standard:** Only organizations with verifiable 2024–2026 active programs are rated High or Medium. Weak evidence is flagged explicitly.

---

## 1. Executive Summary

- **Malaysia's solar buildout creates structural demand for O&M intelligence.** With 5,777 MW installed and LSS5 adding up to 2 GW, the country needs credible yield-monitoring and cleaning-optimisation tools. SolarGuard's core IP addresses the highest-cost problem (dust vs. weather yield loss) that every Malaysian LSS operator faces.

- **IFRS S2 mandatory disclosures from FY2025 (large KLSE Main Market issuers) create a regulatory pull.** LSS operators that are GLC subsidiaries (TNB Renewables, GSPARX, Gentari) or Bursa-listed (Pekat, Solarvest, Samaiden, Cypark) must now quantify climate-related operational risks, including renewable asset yield losses — directly creating a procurement rationale for SolarGuard's ESG data outputs.

- **The government grant landscape is real and accessible.** Cradle CIP SPRINT (up to RM 600K), MRANTI SRF (up to 100% grant for spinoffs), and MOSTI PEMACU 2.0 (up to RM 10M for SRF, up to RM 4M for TeD) are open or recently relaunched. UM patent PI 2024000995 and the UM spinoff structure are strong eligibility levers for 100% non-dilutive grants.

- **The GLC and EPCC channel is the fastest route to revenue.** Pekat (FY2025 revenue doubled to RM 609.9M, profit +105%), Solarvest (RM 1.7B order book), Samaiden (RM 521M order book), and Progressture Solar are all expanding O&M portfolios rapidly — and none currently deploy a domestic AI-soiling classification tool.

- **The VC landscape is cautious but targeted instruments exist.** Gobi Dana Impak Ventures (GDIV), KMP (explicit renewable energy & green tech mandate), and MTDC (commercial equity funds for deeptech) are the most fundable VC paths. Jelawang Capital and the broader Khazanah Fund-of-Funds ecosystem represent an indirect but growing channel.

## 2. Ranked Top 10 Most Realistic Targets

### Rank 1 — MRANTI (Strategic Research Fund, SRF)
**Category:** Government agency / deeptech commercialization grant

MRANTI's SRF explicitly lists "Clean Energy, Green Energy, Renewable Energy" under MOSTI Priority Areas and provides a **100% grant for university spinoffs** up to 36 months duration. SolarGuard's IP is held at UM, meaning a UM spinoff entity qualifies for the 100% grant track (vs. 65% for SMEs). The sensor hardware (BPW34/GL5516/ESP32) and ML classification model sit squarely at TRL 4–7, which is the SRF target band. The SRF application window was January–February 2025 (last cycle) and MRANTI operates rolling review cadences.

**Entry:** Apply via techcomm@mranti.my; pitch as a UM spinoff with a working product and patent.
**Named program:** Strategic Research Fund (SRF), Global Accelerator Programme (GAP)
**Sources:** mranti.my/srf; mranti.my/innovators/grow/gap

---

### Rank 2 — Cradle Fund (CIP SPRINT)
**Category:** Government commercialization grant

CIP SPRINT provides up to **RM 600,000 for 18 months** for early-stage tech commercialization. SolarGuard as a Sdn Bhd (university spinoff) under 7 years old with IP rights is directly eligible. The grant covers product enhancement, market access, and pilot expansion — exactly what a 90-day RM 35–50K pilot programme requires to scale. Cradle has disbursed RM 264M+ to 1,000+ startups and is actively reviewing applications year-round via its Grant Management System. CIP SPRINT does not require equity surrender.

**Entry:** Online application via cradle.com.my, pitch deck submission, 2–4 months to approval.
**Named program:** CIP SPRINT (RM 600K, commercialization); CIP SPARK (RM 150K, pre-seed if earlier stage)
**Sources:** cradle.com.my/cip-sprint; vulcanpost.com/889888/cradle-fund-malaysia-agency-2025-history

### Rank 3 — Pekat Group Berhad (Bursa: 0233)
**Category:** Solar O&M / EPCC company — paid pilot partner

Pekat is the best-fit anchor pilot customer. FY2025 revenue doubled to RM 609.9M; solar PV division up 97%; 1H2025 PAT tripled to RM 26.3M (+189%). The Group is building two 29.99 MWac CGPP solar plants as IPP assets — meaning it will own and operate them for 25 years and needs long-term yield optimization tools. Pekat's shift from pure EPCC to asset-owner/operator (IPP income from FY2027) creates direct SaaS subscription demand.

ROI narrative for pitch: if soiling causes 4–7% yield loss on 5MW at RM 0.35/kWh, recovered revenue = RM 69–171K/year. Across Pekat's 195 MWp portfolio at 60% penetration, that is RM 2.7–6.7M/year in recoverable yield — a clear payback case for RM 35–50K pilot pricing.

**Entry:** Direct business development to Pekat Solar Sdn Bhd operations team. Cold outreach must reach operations/asset management level, not IR.
**Sources:** thestar.com.my (FY2025 results Feb 2026); businesstoday.com.my/2025/08/22 (1H25 results)

### Rank 4 — Khazanah Dana Impak / Gobi Dana Impak Ventures (GDIV)
**Category:** Impact investment VC (Khazanah-backed)

Dana Impak is a RM 6B five-year programme across six themes including "Food and Energy Security" and "Building Climate Resilience." The Gobi Dana Impak Ventures Fund (GDIV) specifically targets Malaysian deeptech startups with national impact potential. Gobi's July 2025 investment in NanoSkunkWorkX (clean energy + semiconductors) via GDIV proves the mandate is active. SolarGuard's combination of UM patent IP, ML/AI classification, ESG data outputs, and GCC expansion ambition closely matches GDIV's criteria ("advanced manufacturing, clean energy, semiconductors").

Jelawang Capital (the national Fund-of-Funds launched October 2024, RM 1B initial allocation) is the LP above GDIV.

**Entry:** Warm introduction through UMCIE → Gobi Partners KL office; present as a UM deeptech spinoff with commercial pilot traction. Alternative: MRANTI GAP accelerator demo day → Gobi network.
**Named program:** Gobi Dana Impak Ventures Fund (GDIV); Future Malaysia Programme
**Sources:** gobi.vc/gobi-dana-impak; khazanah.com.my/dana-impak; gobi.vc/gobi-partners-leads-investment-in-nanoskunkworkx (Jul 2025)

### Rank 5 — Solarvest Holdings Berhad (Bursa: 0215)
**Category:** Solar EPCC / O&M — channel partner + paid pilot

Solarvest has a RM 1.7B order book (Dec 2025), the Malakoff Silver Solar 470MW LSS5 EPCC contract, and a 690MW LSS5+ pipeline. Its O&M division and stated ambitions to build asset management and Fintech services around solar are directly aligned with SolarGuard. Solarvest already partnered with Gentari for 300+ PETRONAS station solar installations. As operator of major LSS assets, Solarvest needs a credible soiling-classification tool to prove yield performance to project finance lenders and ESG auditors.

**Entry:** MOU approach through Solarvest's O&M/Asset Management division; position as an enabling technology for ESG reporting under IFRS S2.
**Sources:** bernama.com (RM 1.06B Malakoff contract Apr 2024); technode.global/2024/03/05 (Gentari partnership)

### Rank 6 — Kumpulan Modal Perdana (KMP)
**Category:** Government-backed VC (MOF / MOSTI)

KMP manages RM 322.5M and explicitly lists "Renewable Energy and Green Technology" as core investment sectors alongside E&E and deeptech. Its February 2026 investment in Econili (lithium battery recycling for the EV/RE value chain) confirms active deployment into clean energy adjacents. KMP is wholly owned by the Ministry of Finance but governed under MOSTI, making it structurally aligned with SolarGuard's UM patent origin.

### Rank 7 — TNB Renewables (TRe) / GSPARX
**Category:** GLC strategic pilot partner + procurement route

TNB has committed RM 42.9B capex for energy transition and won 700MWp+ in LSS5. TNB Renewables (TRe) already partnered with Envision Digital to deploy AIoT-based monitoring on its 50MW Sepang solar farm, explicitly building a "digital renewable energy platform." GSPARX secured 530MW of rooftop solar by end-2025. Both entities are subject to mandatory IFRS S2 reporting from FY2025 as large KLSE Main Market issuers.

SolarGuard can approach as an add-on to existing SCADA/monitoring stacks, providing the dust-vs-weather classification layer that Envision Digital's EnOS does not natively offer.

**Entry:** TNB Group Innovation or TRe's Operations unit; GSPARX commercial partnerships team; procurement tender route for digital services.
**Sources:** eco-business.com (TRe-Envision Digital partnership); solarquarter.com/2025/04/25 (TNB RM 42.9B capex); businesstodaymalaysia.substack.com (GSPARX 530MW)

### Rank 8 — MIDA (Green Investment Strategy / GITA)
**Category:** Regulatory / investment facilitation

MIDA manages the GITA Project (100% GITA on solar EPCC costs, offset against 70% statutory income, valid January 2024–December 2026) and GITE Solar Leasing (70% tax exemption, extended to December 2026). MIDA's Green Investment Strategy (GIS, launched August 2024) under NIMP 2030 includes Renewable Energy as one of seven levers, creating grant and incentive pathways for green tech companies with export potential.

MIDA does not fund SolarGuard directly, but SolarGuard's sensor hardware components can qualify for GITA, and MIDA introductions to project financiers are valuable.

**Entry:** Apply for GITA on hardware sensor components; engage MIDA's Green Technology Division for investor introduction; pitch at IGEM (next cycle 2026).
**Named program:** Green Investment Strategy (GIS); Green Investment Tax Allowance (GITA); IGEM
**Sources:** mida.gov.my/industries/services/green-technology; mida.gov.my (IGEM 2025 RM 7.3B)

### Rank 9 — Samaiden Group Berhad (Bursa: 0274)
**Category:** Solar EPCC / O&M — paid pilot partner

Samaiden's order book hit a record RM 521.2M in 2024, with CGPP and utility-scale solar at 54% of the book. As an O&M provider that also owns projects, Samaiden is acquiring long-term yield risk. Samaiden's May 2025 RM 45M Kulim solar project and CGPP contracts give it operational farms where SolarGuard's 90-day pilot can demonstrate ROI. Unlike Pekat (stronger EPCC focus), Samaiden's O&M practice is a primary revenue line, making it a natural recurring SaaS customer.

**Entry:** Direct business development to Samaiden's O&M division; frame as an ESG-data-generation tool for sustainability reporting.
**Sources:** insage.com.my/BursaNews (record order book Oct 2024); nst.com.my/business/corporate/2025/05 (Kulim contract)

### Rank 10 — MOSTI PEMACU 2.0 (SRF / TeD track)
**Category:** Government R&D-to-commercialization grant

PEMACU 2.0 launched February 2026 with RM 100M allocated. SRF provides up to RM 10M; TeD provides up to RM 4M. The five named priority sectors do NOT explicitly include solar O&M — this is a significant limitation. However, the SRF track under DANA PEMACU covers renewable energy as a MOSTI Priority Area (separate from the five PEMACU 2.0 pillars), and the fund is open to university spinoffs for 100% grant. **Eligibility confirmation required before applying** — verify with MOSTI that "solar O&M monitoring" qualifies under the renewable energy track.

**Entry:** MOSTI Sistem Dana Bersepadu (SDB) portal at sdb.mosti.gov.my; UMCIE should co-apply as the patent-holding institution.
**Named program:** Dana PEMACU 2.0 — Strategic Research Fund (SRF) / Technology Development Fund (TeD)
**Sources:** bernama.com/en/news.php?id=2522684; wargabiz.com.my/2026/02/25

## 4. Best First 90-Day Outreach Plan

### Week 1–2: Internal groundwork (UMCIE)
- Schedule meeting with UMCIE (umcie@um.edu.my / 03-7967 7351) to confirm:
  - Spinoff Sdn Bhd formation readiness (must be ≥51% Malaysian-owned)
  - Patent licensing terms for PI 2024000995 (IP stays with UM; spinoff gets exclusive licence)
  - Grant co-application eligibility for SRF and Cradle CIP SPRINT
- Confirm SolarSense will be structured as a Sdn Bhd with UM as IP licensor — this unlocks the 100% grant track at MRANTI SRF.
- Prepare one-page commercial traction summary: pilot site identified (Pekat or Samaiden), patent filed, TRL 6–7 working demo.

### Week 3–4: Apply to Cradle CIP SPRINT
- Submit online at cradle.com.my using Cradle's pitch deck template.
- Target: up to RM 600,000 for 18 months. Expected approval: 2–4 months.
- Parallel: Contact MRANTI (techcomm@mranti.my) to confirm SRF next application window and eligibility of "solar O&M monitoring" under the MOSTI Clean Energy track.

### Week 5–6: Approach Pekat Group for paid pilot
- Send one-page business case to Pekat Solar Sdn Bhd operations team (not IR).
- Frame: 90-day pilot (RM 35–50K) as a yield-recovery proof-of-concept on one of their CGPP 29.99 MWac assets.
- ROI narrative: 4–7% soiling loss at RM 0.35/kWh on 5MW = RM 69–171K/year recovered. Across their 195 MWp portfolio, this is the argument for a long-term SaaS subscription.

### Week 7–8: Approach Samaiden O&M division
- Parallel outreach to Samaiden's O&M services team.
- Position SolarGuard as an IFRS S2 ESG data tool (yield reporting for sustainability auditors) rather than a cost-reduction tool — Samaiden's CGPP assets are under performance guarantees.
- Request a meeting with Samaiden's O&M Director.

### Week 9–10: Warm introduction to Gobi Partners (GDIV)
- Request UMCIE to facilitate introduction to Gobi Partners KL via the Khazanah / Dana Impak ecosystem.
- Prepare a 10-slide investor deck (see Section 5 below).
- Alternative path: Apply for MRANTI GAP 2025 accelerator (applications open July–November cycle) to enter Gobi's network via MRANTI demo day.

### Week 11–12: MIDA registration + KMP direct approach
- Register with MIDA's Green Technology Division for GITA assessment on sensor hardware components — this creates a procurement incentive for SolarGuard's early customers.
- Contact KMP directly at kmp.vc with one-page executive summary. Frame as: deeptech + renewable energy + UM spinoff — exactly their mandate.

**Priority sequence:**
1. Cradle CIP SPRINT (fastest non-dilutive capital, 2–4 months)
2. MRANTI SRF (highest grant value, 100% for spinoffs)
3. Pekat Group paid pilot (revenue + proof-of-concept)
4. Samaiden paid pilot (O&M-specific, ESG angle)
5. Gobi GDIV / KMP (equity, 6–9 months horizon)

## 5. Evidence and Deck Materials Required Before Approaching

### For all grant applications (Cradle, MRANTI SRF, PEMACU 2.0):
- UM spinoff Sdn Bhd incorporation documents (≥51% Malaysian-owned, ≤7 years old)
- Patent certificate or filing confirmation for PI 2024000995 (UM IP)
- TRL assessment document — target TRL 6–7 (system demonstrated in relevant environment)
- ML classifier performance: accuracy rate of dust vs. weather classification on Malaysian meteorological and irradiance data
- 3-day forecasting backtest: LinearRegression RMSE vs. actual on simulated and/or pilot data
- ROI model with named site data: MWp, RM/kWh, soiling loss %, cleaning cost vs. yield recovery
- UM supervisor endorsement letter
- Completed business plan in Cradle/MRANTI template

### For pilot partners (Pekat, Samaiden, Solarvest, Progressture):
- One-page executive summary + ROI calculator for 5MW reference farm
- 90-day pilot proposal: scope, deliverables, pricing (RM 35–50K), site requirements (sensor installation, data access, API credentials)
- Proof of sensor functionality: BPW34/GL5516 array data vs. weather station reference
- UM patent PI 2024000995 reference (credibility signal for a listed-company partner)
- IFRS S2 output description: what reports SolarGuard generates for ESG auditors
- Early-adopter commercial terms: data confidentiality, IP protection, co-publication rights

### For VC investors (Gobi GDIV, KMP, MTDC):
- 10-slide investor deck:
  1. Problem (soiling loss in Malaysian LSS — RM 27,921/MW/year from Sulaiman et al. 2018 Perak)
  2. Solution (RandomForest ML classifier + UM patent sensor + IEC 61724 measurement protocol)
  3. Traction (pilot LOI or signed agreement with named O&M company)
  4. Market size (RM-denominated: 5,777 MW installed, 117 LSS holders, RM 500K–1M ARR target Month 36)
  5. Moat (UM patent + proprietary local training dataset + IEC 61724 calibration baseline)
  6. Business model (RM 6–10/kWp/year SaaS; 90-day pilot RM 35–50K; Tier 2 hardware)
  7. GCC expansion roadmap (Months 25–36: UAE, Saudi, Kuwait at 1.55× pricing)
  8. Team and advisors
  9. Financial projections (3-year: ARR growth, gross margin ~91%, LTV:CAC benchmarks)
  10. Ask + use of funds (18-month runway to first annual contract and second channel partner)
- Cap table showing UM IP licensing terms
- Letter of intent / MOU from at least one pilot partner (Pekat or Samaiden)
- GCC market sizing for Months 25–36 expansion
